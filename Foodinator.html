01 — About

About Foodinator

A two-chef kitchen, a 36-seat dining room, and a soundtrack that runs from city pop to late-90s anime ED themes. Foodinator opened in 2024 with one idea: serve the food we always wanted to eat after a long shift, in a room we always wanted to sit in.

Fusion that actually fuses.

Chef Mei trained in Osaka. Chef Kenji grew up in his family's Seoul fried-chicken joint. The menu sits at the intersection — handmade ramen finished with gochujang butter, karaage tossed in a yuzu glaze, dumplings filled with miso-braised short rib.

The dining room nods to the kitchens we cooked our way through as kids — paper lanterns, a hand-painted mural of the alleyway in Spirited Away, leather booths the colour of tonkatsu sauce. It's a restaurant first, a love letter second.
